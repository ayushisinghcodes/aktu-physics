1. Interference in Thin Films

When light strikes a very thin transparent film (like a soap bubble or oil slick), it undergoes partial reflection and partial transmission at the top and bottom surfaces. These reflected and transmitted waves superimpose to produce interference patterns.

Path Difference in Reflected Light

For a film of thickness \( t \) and refractive index \( \mu \), light reflected from the lower surface travels an extra path inside the film. In addition, there is an abrupt phase change of \( \pi \) (equivalent to a path change of \( \lambda/2 \)) when reflection occurs from a denser medium.

The effective path difference \( \Delta x \) is:

$$ \Delta x = 2 \mu t \cos r \pm \frac{\lambda}{2} $$

Where \( r \) is the angle of refraction inside the film.

Condition for Maxima (Constructive Interference / Bright Fringe)

$$ 2 \mu t \cos r \pm \frac{\lambda}{2} = n\lambda \implies 2 \mu t \cos r = (2n - 1)\frac{\lambda}{2} $$

\( n = 1, 2, 3, \dots \)

Condition for Minima (Destructive Interference / Dark Fringe)

$$ 2 \mu t \cos r \pm \frac{\lambda}{2} = (2n - 1)\frac{\lambda}{2} \implies 2 \mu t \cos r = n\lambda $$

\( n = 0, 1, 2, \dots \)

2. Newton's Rings

Newton's rings are formed due to interference between light waves reflected from the top and bottom surfaces of the air film bounded between an plano-convex lens and a plane glass plate.

Diameter of Rings

Let \( R \) be the radius of curvature of the plano-convex lens and \( r_n \) be the radius of the \(n\)-th ring.

The thickness \( t \) of the air film at a distance \( r_n \) from the center is given by \( t = \frac{r_n^2}{2R} \).

For normal incidence, \( r = 0 \) and \( \cos r = 1 \). For an air film, \( \mu = 1 \).

Diameter of \(n\)-th dark ring (\( D_n = 2 r_n \)):

$$ 2 t = n\lambda \implies 2 \left( \frac{D_n^2}{8R} \right) = n\lambda \implies D_n^2 = 4nR\lambda $$ $$ D_n = \sqrt{4nR\lambda} \propto \sqrt{n} $$

Diameter of \(n\)-th bright ring:

$$ 2 t = (2n-1)\frac{\lambda}{2} \implies D_n = \sqrt{2(2n-1)R\lambda} \propto \sqrt{\text{odd numbers}} $$

3. Diffraction of Light

Diffraction is the phenomenon of bending of light waves around the edges of an obstacle or an aperture, whose dimensions are comparable to the wavelength of light.

Fraunhofer vs. Fresnel Diffraction

In Fraunhofer Diffraction, the source and the screen are effectively at infinite distances from the aperture (achieved using convex lenses). The wavefront falling on the aperture is planar.

In Fresnel Diffraction, the source or the screen or both are at finite distances from the aperture without the use of lenses. The wavefront is spherical or cylindrical.

Mathematical Derivations

4. Fraunhofer Diffraction at a Single Slit

Consider a monochromatic plane wave of wavelength \( \lambda \) incident normally on a slit of width \( e \). The secondary wavelets from all points within the slit interfere at a point P on a distant screen.

The path difference between the wavelets originating from the extreme edges of the slit is:

$$ \text{Path Difference} = e \sin\theta $$

The resultant intensity \( I \) at angle \( \theta \) is given by:

$$ I = I_0 \left( \frac{\sin \alpha}{\alpha} \right)^2 \quad \text{where} \quad \alpha = \frac{\pi e \sin\theta}{\lambda} $$

Conditions for Minima

$$ e \sin\theta_n = \pm n\lambda \quad \text{where } n = 1, 2, 3, \dots $$

The central maximum is flanked by minima symmetrically situated on either side.

5. Numerical Problems (AKTU Pattern)

Q1: Newton's Rings

Problem: In a Newton's rings experiment, the diameter of the 15th dark ring was found to be 0.590 cm and that of the 5th ring was 0.336 cm. If the radius of the plano-convex lens is 100 cm, calculate the wavelength of light used.

Solution:

Using the formula for the diameter of dark rings:

$$ \lambda = \frac{D_{m+n}^2 - D_m^2}{4nR} $$

Given: \(m=5\), \(m+n=15 \implies n=10\). And \(R = 100 \text{ cm}\).

\(D_{15} = 0.590 \text{ cm}\), \(D_5 = 0.336 \text{ cm}\).

$$ \lambda = \frac{(0.590)^2 - (0.336)^2}{4 \times 10 \times 100} = \frac{0.3481 - 0.112896}{4000} $$ $$ \lambda \approx \frac{0.2352}{4000} \text{ cm} \approx 5.88 \times 10^{-5} \text{ cm} = 5880 \text{ \AA} $$

Answer: The wavelength is approximately \(5880 \text{ \AA}\).
